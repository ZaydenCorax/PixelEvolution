It also carries a hover cell-inspector tooltip and leaves shelf-space for the planned economy layer.

Why this works
  • Controls and readout are permanently docked — nothing floats over the play area, so the sim stays legible.
  • Best fit for the planned economy: the right rail already frames an Upgrades shelf and the top bar has EVO/GT slots.
Trade-off
  • Two rails narrow the grid more than a full-bleed layout would. Great for a "control room" feel, less for immersion.
WHAT CHANGED
01
Grid reclaims the frame
Was a small off-center square in a sea of empty dark. Now centered and enlarged in every layout.
02
Pheromones as faded breadcrumbs
Kept the per-cell shrinking-square crumbs from the original, but split into two faded hues: blue = home trail, pink = food trail. Each square shrinks and fades with age, so ants (bright discs) never blur into them.
03
Ants read as bright dots
White / lime / orange discs by state, haloed so they pop above the crumbs — no more blue-on-blue confusion.
04
A real transport bar
Play/Pause, single-step, speed 1×/2×/4×, and restart — replacing the lone Pause button.
05
Seed surfaced & editable
The mulberry32 seed is shown, editable, and re-rollable for reproducible runs.
06
Stats integrated + legible
Each state row gets an energy bar and count; a population meter shows headroom to the cap. Docked into the frame, not floating loose.
07
Hover tooltip, designed
Cell coords, terrain, food, both pheromone levels, and ant count — the requested per-cell inspector.
08
Economy-ready shell
EVO / GT currency slots in the top bar and a reserved (locked) Upgrades panel so the idle layer has a home when it lands.
